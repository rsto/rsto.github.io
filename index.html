about me

I am rsto, a software developer mainly working on network services, systems engineering and character encodings. In real life, my name is Robert Stepanek and I'm based in Vienna, Austria.

what I am currently doing

Currently, I’m happy to contract at FastMail, where I am one of the major contributors to the Cyrus IMAP project. It’s all open-source in my Github repo. Also sponsored by FastMail, I am member of the CalConnect Calendaring Consortium, where I work on defining a new calendaring data model. Go check out our IETF RFC draft.

what I have done previously

Most of my previous code is proprietary and notably tons of it run in the provisioning systems of a large Austrian telecommunications provider.

Still, in the last two decades I found time to contribute anything from one-liners up to klocs to at least the following open-source projects: Xapian, x/net/webdav, libical, libxml, SabreDAV. I also vaguely recall having contributed a bug fix to ffmpeg, GNU Hurd and being the sole user of my custom bootloader for a SGI Personal Iris.

Besides churning code, I worked about six years as a business consultant in telecommunications at one of the Big 5 consulting firms. I also launched a few business ventures, all of which the dust of time has settled on.

In 2004 I graduated as MSc in Computer Science at University of Technology Vienna (TU Wien) writing the modeler code for photorealistic ocean waves at the Computer Graphics group. In 2006 I worked as research assistant at Department of Theoretical Informatics at University of Technology Munich (TUM).

In 1998 I co-founded a free web host and ran it over a decade. We hosted our own radio show and organized free Linux workshops.
