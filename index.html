what I have done previously

Most of my previous code is proprietary and notably tons of it run in the provisioning systems of a large Austrian telecommunications provider.

Still, in the last two decades I found time to contribute anything from one-liners up to considerable lines of code to at least the following open-source projects:

  • the Xapian search engine
  • Go's x/net/webdav
  • the RFC 5545 iCalendar library libical
  • the libxml C library
  • the SabreDAV DAV framework
I also vaguely recall having contributed a bug fix to ffmpeg, porting Debian packages to GNU Hurd and being the sole user of my custom bootloader for a SGI Personal Iris.

Besides churning code, I worked about six years as a business consultant in telecommunications and online media at Accenture. I also launched a few business ventures, all of which the dust of time has settled on.

In 2004 I graduated as MSc in Computer Science at University of Technology, Vienna (TU Wien), writing the modeler code for photorealistic ocean waves at the Computer Graphics group. In 2006 I worked as research assistant at the department of Theoretical Informatics at University of Technology Munich (TUM).

In 1998 I co-founded the free web host lo-res.org and ran it over a decade. We hosted our own radio show and organized free Linux workshops.
